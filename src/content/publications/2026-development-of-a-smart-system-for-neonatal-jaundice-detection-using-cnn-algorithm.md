In the majority of low and middle-income nations, newborn jaundice is a major factor in neonatal admissions and mortality. It causes a baby's skin and eyes to turn yellow. Jaundice in newborns is fairly prevalent and can happen when bilirubin levels are high in newborns. Increased bilirubin, if untreated, can harm the brain. To identify babies who are at risk for hyperbilirubinemia, early jaundice prediction has become increasingly important. In order to assist the health sector, our research is proposing a method to develop a non-invasive method for detecting jaundice on a regular basis to estimate bilirubin. Therefore, we suggest a machine learning approach for non-invasive early neonatal jaundice prediction.
